About Me

Programmer & Graphic Designer

My name is Ezeme Chukwudubem, a passionate Frontend Developer and Graphics Designer based in Nigeria. I love transforming creative ideas into interactive, user-friendly web experiences. My curiosity for technology began at an early age, and over time I’ve grown into a detail-oriented developer who enjoys crafting clean, accessible interfaces. Outside coding, I enjoy music, design, and learning how visual storytelling influences user interaction.

My Goals

As a Frontend Developer and Graphics Designer, my primary goal is to create visually appealing and user-friendly web applications that provide seamless user experiences. I aim to continuously enhance my skills in modern web technologies, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and popular frameworks like React and Vue.js. Additionally, I strive to stay updated with the latest design trends and best practices to ensure that my designs are not only aesthetically pleasing but also functional and accessible. Ultimately, I aspire to contribute to innovative projects that make a positive impact on users while advancing my career in the tech industry.

Areas with Low Confidence

And how I’m actively improving in my areas of low confidence.

Advanced JavaScript Logic

I sometimes struggle to break down complex functions or handle nested conditions effectively.

My Improvement Plan

I’m taking daily 30-minute coding challenges and revisiting problem-solving patterns using freeCodeCamp and LeetCode.

Mastering Responsive Design.

Designs sometimes break on smaller screens or look inconsistent across devices.

My Improvement Plan

I’m improving by practicing mobile-first design, using CSS Grid and Flexbox consistently, and testing layouts on multiple screen sizes with browser dev tools.

Public Speaking & Technical Presentation

I find it challenging to convey my ideas clearly and confidently in front of an audience.

My Improvement Plan

I’m working on improving my confidence by rehearsing my project walkthroughs, recording myself speaking, and joining small online dev communities where I can share what I build.

Networking and Collaboration

I often feel hesitant to reach out or collaborate with other developers, fearing that my skills might not measure up or that I might slow others down.

My Improvement Plan

I’m working on building confidence by joining online developer communities, participating in open-source projects, and asking for feedback. Each collaboration helps me learn, improve, and see the value of teamwork in tech growth.
